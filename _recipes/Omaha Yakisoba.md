Preheat a large pan over medium heat and add 1 tablespoon of oil. Sauté the broccoli in the oil for about 5 minutes until bright green. Add splashes of water if it seems to get dry. Add the cabbage and protein, and cook for about 3 minutes, until the cabbage is wilted but still a little snappy. Add the corn and toss to warm through.

Push all the veggies aside and add the garlic and ginger to the pan. Quickly drizzle the remaining 1 teaspoon of oil over the garlic and ginger, then toss for a few seconds, until fragrant; then mix in among the rest of the veggies.

Add the soy sauce, broth, mirin, and sriracha to the pan and toss to coat. Let that cook down for about 1 minute. Add the noodles and toss again to coat. Last, toss in the scallions, and then serve.

# Notes
- Make sure the pan is plenty hot! Everything should sizzle as it touches down. Flick a little water from your fingers into the pan to test once it's been preheated; the water should evaporate almost immediately.
- You can buy ready-to-eat udon noodles in the refrigerated section of many supermarkets. They are so convenient! I run them under warm water for a minute just to get them to separate and then toss 'em right into the pan. But read the package directions and follow them, just in case they're a bit different.
- You can also, of course, boil your own noodles -- 4 ounces ought to do it. Since udon comes in many varieties, make sure you get the thick kind. Otherwise, I feel you'd be missing out.
- This recipe serves two -- any more ingredients than that won't fit in the pan at the same time. So if you'd like to double the recipe, it's so incredibly fast that you can just make two separate batches. If you prep all the veggies for both batches together, I bet you can still get everything on the table in 30 minutes.
